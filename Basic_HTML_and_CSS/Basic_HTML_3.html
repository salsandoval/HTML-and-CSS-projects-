If you’re the person in your household who’s largely responsible for grocery shopping, there’s little doubt you’ve seen them — clear glass bottles filled with liquid refreshment in eye-catching, mouth-watering shades of orange, green, yellow and red. Chances are you’ve tried one as well, and maybe even a flavor (Guava? Mango? Jamaica?) you never imagined would make sense in soda form. Either way, you’ve encountered a brand that has carved out more than a niche in the crowded soda marketplace, and whose appeal cuts across traditional demographic categories: Jarritos. But how did Jarritos come to be such a familiar sight in North American supermarkets? From the beverage’s humble beginnings in a Mexican cocina to its current status as something of a household name, the story of Jarritos is every bit as colorful as their naturally flavored sodas and distinctively molded glass bottles.
